A Not-So-Sell-Researched Speculative Essay on Tech

Aron Ferreras


Summary:

Take a deep dive into the past and present to hypothesize what our future may look like!


Notes:

As we rapidly approach the end of a hectic year of insane tech evaluations leading to a large amount of money being put into tech and the many claims of AI dominance in all markets, I thought it would be a good idea to go back and think about previous technological developments that completely changed the landscape of how we perceived technology. Then, after reminiscing about the past, we look towards the future to take a stab at predicting what the next 50 years of technology would look like. As is the case with predictions such as these, no matter how wide of a net we cast, we might as well be in the wrong body of water for the fish! Basically, there’s no way of knowing how right we are, even if we reference past evidence. Additionally, this is purely based on my understanding of these topics, as if I’m taking an in person essay writing test - no time for research!

I believe that technology as a whole aims to better the lives of people who face various issues. The people that face these issues may be singular, and the technology could end up cascading into something else entirely, but its conception is birthed from one person trying to come up with some sort of solution. We saw the invention of vehicles to solve the issue of transportation or thermal regulation for warmth during the winter and even toilets and the sewage system being designed to take waste and move it elsewhere! However, I believe the most consequential issue that cascaded into something even greater was handling data. Researchers with mountains of information who needed to access it throughout various parts of the world faced this issue of getting this information handled in a timely manner. Sure, there were phonelines in place, but this process was slow and inefficient. The solution? Invent the internet.

The internet was incredibly consequential! Its initial creation as a way to transport data for others to access was soon available to the public, and almost instantly, gears started to shift and change was explosive. We had people from all over the world getting access to information that was normally only available in books or by knowing some guy who knows some other guy! It was incredible! So incredible, that people started to really invest a lot of time and money into every aspect of the internet. From domain names to conceptual purposes of sites, everyone and their mothers were trying to buy a piece of this new internet in order to flip it and make more money by the end of it. Unfortunately, this led to the infamous “Dotcom bubble”, which led to a large loss of revenue, but it did cause the market to refocus and start investing into essential tech businesses.

Shortly after the development of the internet, we see major advancements in how we’re able to access the internet and what the internet could be used for. Many of the developments at this time were centered around the internet - smartphones, web browsers, multiplayer gaming - but even still, issues persisted. The issue at this time was how slowly things moved, and since time equals money, this meant that the slower things were, the more money was being lost. To fix this problem, we entered an era of optimization, where we try to make tech that’s much faster, more efficient, and much more affordable to manufacture. While not a singular innovation was made that really sparked a revolution, many miniature optimizations have led to the tech today to be incredibly quick, to the point where quantum computers are being talked about as if they’re sure to happen. These speeds for computation were so impressive that people were able to create algorithms that compute so quickly in order to give nearly human-like responses, and thus artificial intelligence enters the space.

AI is not technically a new invention, but it’s a far cry from what it once was. What was once a technology invented to solve the issue of data handling, is now a system that’s incredibly advanced. We’re seeing algorithms mimic human-like texting, human speech patterns, human image creation, human video creation… Essentially, we’re seeing AI mimic being human. Their likeness to humanity is so astonishing, so convincing, that many people and their mothers have found it worthwhile to invest in. Money in the big B Billions is being thrown around by people within the tech industry that it feels like an incredible, no-brainer investment to make. Surely, with a growth this strong, this bubble doesn’t pop, right?

And with that, we’re looking ahead into the future. Not just a year ahead, or two, or ten, and not even twenty years from now. No, we’re looking well beyond all of these milestones, towards the fifty year mark. What could be in store for us then? Well, despite my feigned cluelessness earlier, there most definitely is a high likelihood that the AI bubble dissipates in some way, where a lot of the fat and excess gets sloughed off, leaving behind a framework that is bound to be incredibly essential to the next ground breaking technology. This system of algorithmic machine learning is already providing incredible advancements to medical fields, so it’s likely that it’ll continue to proceed in this way. We may see a quicker consolidation of information, and rapid access to things we continue to search for. After all, time is limited and we need to make the most of it. We can’t let even one second of our lives be taken over by the process of searching through mountains of information.

Not only is there going to be essentially a pinpoint access hub for all information for people to easily access a la Wikipedia, there’s also going to be a democratization of the current internet to where anyone and everyone can easily access the internet on any device with incredible speeds. I think there’s also a likelihood that the current internet will also see an evaporation of its current framework, where bigger interests and corporations currently fuel sites with advertisements. Instead, the internet will see new formations of laws that aim to regulate its various aspects from different groups of people. We’re hurdling into a situation where many new issues are sprouting because of the internet, and so inventions related to the internet are bound to pop up in the coming years. However, only time will tell when we see these changes occurring.
